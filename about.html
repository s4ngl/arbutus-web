The Arbutus Yearbook

The Arbutus yearbook is a student-run publication under the umbrella of Indiana University Student Media. Since 1894, we have been capturing the memories and stories that define the Indiana University experience.

Our History

Founded in 1894, the Arbutus has been documenting campus life for over 130 years. Our name comes from the arbutus flower, which was once abundant on the Indiana University campus.

Throughout the decades, we have chronicled the evolution of student life, campus traditions, academic achievements, and the vibrant community that makes IU special.

Today, we continue this rich tradition with modern storytelling techniques while maintaining the timeless quality that makes yearbooks treasured keepsakes.

Quick Facts

  • Founded: 1894
  • Published: Annually
  • Staff: Student-run
  • Organization: Indiana University Student Media
  • Mission: Documenting campus life and student experiences

Our Mission & Values

Documentation

We capture the moments, people, and events that define each academic year at Indiana University.

Storytelling

Through words and images, we tell the stories that matter to our campus community.

Tradition

We honor the legacy of over 130 years while embracing innovation and modern techniques.

Indiana University Student Media

The Arbutus is proud to be part of Indiana University Student Media, which provides students with hands-on experience in journalism, photography, and publishing.

Professional Development

Students gain real-world experience in journalism, design, photography, and project management while working on an award-winning publication.

Award-Winning Publication

The Arbutus has received recognition for excellence in student journalism and yearbook design, showcasing the talent and dedication of our staff.
